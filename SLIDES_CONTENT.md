## Slide 3: Three Fundamental Principles
**Variant:** Dark  
**Title:** Three Fundamental Principles  
**Image:** standing-wave.png (right side, 60/40 layout)

### Standing Waves
When a vibrating wire is trapped between two fixed points, waves bounce back and forth and stack on top of each other, creating spots that barely move and spots that vibrate wildly. This pattern only happens at specific frequencies.

### Resonance
When the wire's natural vibration frequency matches the frequency of a tuning fork nearby, energy transfers perfectly and the wire vibrates with maximum amplitude (biggest swings). It's like pushing a swing at exactly the right moment‚Äîsmall pushes create huge motion.

### Wave Speed Relationship
Sound travels through the wire at a speed determined by how tight it is (tension) and how heavy it is (mass). Tighter wires = faster waves = higher pitch; heavier wires = slower waves = lower pitch.
